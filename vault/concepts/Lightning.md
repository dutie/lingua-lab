## Cross-linguistic notes

French and Spanish agree on the two-way split between *the flash* (the light) and *the bolt* (the striking force):

| | flash | bolt |
|---|---|---|
| ES | relámpago | rayo |
| FR | éclair | foudre |

Japanese splits more finely — *稲妻* (literary flash), *雷* (weather), *閃光* (technical). Register matters in Japanese more than in either Romance language.

English collapses all of this into "lightning" and forces you to disambiguate by context. That's why it feels confusing going from EN → ES/FR: you're learning a distinction your native language doesn't mark.

## Faux amis watch

*éclair* and *rayo* are both "lightning-ish" but point at different halves of the concept. Don't let one substitute for the other in your head.
