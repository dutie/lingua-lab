# Inbox

Quick-capture scratch space. When a word jumps out at you on your commute, type it here on your phone — just the word, no definition, no context. Two seconds of typing.

In your evening session, open this folder, take each word, and decide:

1. **Make a full note.** If the word has shown up multiple times, seems useful, or you actively want to remember it. Copy the relevant [[../templates/vocabulary-es|vocab template]], fill it in, add the SR line, delete the inbox entry.
2. **Drop it.** If on reflection it doesn't matter, just delete.

Cap: no more than 10 inbox → full-note conversions per day per language. The cap from [[../PLAN]] applies here too.

## Format

One word per file is fine, but you can also just keep a running daily file like `2026-04-22.md` with a bullet list. Whatever's fastest to type on mobile.

## Why capture at all vs. just remembering

Working memory during your commute is small and the words compete. If you don't capture, you'll either forget or spend mental energy trying not to forget, both of which take you out of the listening. Capture = free the buffer.
