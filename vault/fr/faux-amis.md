# faux amis (FR ↔ ES)

Running list of "false friends" between French and Spanish. Add to this as you encounter them. **No SR tag on this file** — individual faux amis that trip you up hard can get their own SR-tagged notes.

## Actual false friends

| FR | Sounds like ES | But actually means | ES word for that meaning |
|---|---|---|---|
| *attendre* | *atender* | to wait (FR) vs to attend to / serve (ES) | *esperar* / *servir* |
| *sortir* | *surtir* | to go out (FR) vs to supply (ES) | *salir* |
| *enterrer* | *enterar* | to bury (FR) vs to inform (ES) | *sepultar* / *informar* |
| *quitter* | *quitar* | to leave (FR) vs to remove (ES) | *abandonar* / *remover* |
| *embarasser* | *embarazar* | to embarrass (FR) vs to impregnate (ES) | **big one, don't mix these up** |
| *large* | *largo* | wide (FR) vs long (ES) | *ancho* (wide in ES) / *long* (long in FR) |
| *gâteau* | *gato* | cake (FR) vs cat (ES) | *pastel* / *chat* |

## Mostly-true cognates (don't overthink these)

Most -tion/-ción words map directly: *nation / nación*, *station / estación*, *communication / comunicación*. Same for -ment/-mente adverbs: *normalement / normalmente*.

The bridge mostly works. It's the exceptions in the table above that get you.

## When to pull one out for its own SR card

If a faux ami bites you three times in a week, make a dedicated note for just the ES side or just the FR side (whichever language you're actively in), with the false friend flagged in the Notes section. Don't make an SR card that tests "which meaning applies in which language?" — that's translation-mediated retrieval, the thing we avoid.
