# quedar

quedar::to remain / to be left; quedar con = to meet up with #sr/es

## Examples
- *Quedan dos manzanas.* — There are two apples left.
- *Quedamos a las ocho.* — Let's meet at eight.
- *Ese vestido te queda bien.* — That dress suits you.

## Notes

Tricky because the meaning shifts by construction:
- *quedar* alone = to remain / be left
- *quedar con alguien* = to arrange to meet someone
- *quedarse* (reflexive) = to stay in a place, to keep something
- *quedar + adjective* = to end up being / to look (X)

## French bridge

No direct French cognate. Closest maps:
- "to remain" → *rester*
- "to meet up with" → *se donner rendez-vous*, *se retrouver*
- "to suit (clothes)" → *aller à*

So *quedar* is basically a verb that bundles three French verbs under one Spanish root. Worth remembering the bundle rather than translating case-by-case.
